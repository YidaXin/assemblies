# README

This repository is forked from 
[https://github.com/dmitropolsky/assemblies](https://github.com/dmitropolsky/assemblies).

The original work is known as *Assembly Calculus*, see
- https://ccneuro.org/2019/proceedings/0000998.pdf
- https://www.pnas.org/content/117/25/14464

I have decided to improve upon this work because it is reminiscent of an in-depth conversation
that I had discussed with Patrick Henry Winston, upon our first meeting on 31 May 2018. The 
conversation was about implementing Marvin Minsky’s K-Lines idea, where small fixed-sized
_bundles_ of neurons can be considered as what Minsky called “mental agents,” and organizations of
such bundles can be considered as what Minsky called “mental agencies.”

Minsky’s original idea, much like the idea here of the authors of Assembly Calculus, is abstract
and vague. To concretize it, we have to think about specific use cases. Fortunately, both Minsky
and the authors here seem to believe that language is an especially important capability given
rise to by this kind of brain architecture.  The main different, as far as I think, is that the
authors here argue for syntax, while Minsky argued for semantics.  But either way, language seems
to be a good testbed for such an experimental brain-inspired cognitive architecture.

Minsky did propose a special kind of K-Lines that is meant for language learning, which he called
_polynemes_.  In its simplest form, a polyneme is a collection of recognized properties in the
listener's brain when he/she hears some specific entity or event.  For example, when the listener
hears “apple,” the properties of _red_ (color), _round_ (shape), and _sweet_ (taste) are invoked.
Minsky argues that this is because each such property is recognized by a designated “mental agent.”
